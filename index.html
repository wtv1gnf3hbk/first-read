Update: Scientific context published Thursday
Venezuela doublet explained: a 7.2 and 7.5 struck 39 seconds apart on a fault comparable to San Andreas. USGS says the quakes ruptured where the Caribbean plate moves eastward past the South American plate at about 0.79 inches per year. The Bocono fault last produced a doublet in September 2025. Venezuela has no earthquake early-warning system. USGS puts the probability of at least one magnitude-4 aftershock within a week at 99 percent, and a magnitude-6 aftershock at 24 percent. →
