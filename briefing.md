**Iran launches drones and missiles at Bahrain and Kuwait after U.S. strikes; halt deal reached.** Iran's Revolutionary Guard claimed responsibility for drone and missile attacks on Bahrain and Kuwait on Sunday, hours after U.S. Central Command struck 10 Iranian military targets. **Israel cabinet unanimously recognizes Armenian genocide, drawing Turkish and Azerbaijani condemnation.** Israel's cabinet voted unanimously Sunday to recognize the Ottoman-era mass killings of Armenians as genocide. The decision, still requiring parliamentary ratification, reflects the collapse of Israeli-Turkish ties. Turkey called it a politically motivated move. Azerbaijan condemned the decision as "unacceptable." Israeli Foreign Minister Gideon Saar said 32 countries have similarly classified the killings. [link](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/29/g-s1-130772/israel-armenian-wwi-deaths-genocide)

Putin acknowledged Sunday a "certain deficit" of fuel for the first time and said Russia would import more fuel and accelerate repairs. Russia shot down 213 Ukrainian drones overnight; Ukraine downed 125 of 142 Russian drones and 7 of 8 missiles.
