**Venezuela earthquake toll rises to 188 as U.S. pledges $150 million, sends rescue teams.** The Trump administration announced $150 million for relief groups and the United Nations, and mobilized two urban search-and-rescue teams from Fairfax County and Los Angeles. The U.S. military will provide aircraft for damage assessment and aid delivery. Wednesday's 7.2 and 7.5 magnitude quakes destroyed the main Caracas airport, complicating aid logistics. More than 1,500 people were injured and thousands remain missing. [link](https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-earthquake-us-united-states-aid-donations-ebd85d82ef5af24419eb8a4c417b57dc)

**Supreme Court lets Trump end TPS for 350,000 Haitians and 6,000 Syrians in 6-3 ruling.** The conservative majority ruled courts cannot review the process DHS used to revoke Temporary Protected Status, allowing swift termination of protections covering 1.3 million people from 17 countries total. Justice Alito's majority opinion dismissed claims that Trump's comments about Haitians showed racial bias. Justice Kagan dissented, calling those comments 'so repellent and racially inflected that the majority declines to put them in print.' [link](https://apnews.com/article/immigration-supreme-court-haiti-syria-tps-1bbbf8115f984a0d53336656924e989d)

**Supreme Court also clears Trump to revive border metering in second 6-3 immigration ruling.** In a separate Thursday decision, the court overturned a lower-court block on 'metering,' the practice of capping daily asylum applications at ports of entry. The policy was first used under Obama and expanded in Trump's first term. DHS did not say whether it plans to reinstate it, but called the ruling 'an important tool.' Justice Sotomayor read her dissent aloud; Alito publicly rebutted her from the bench. [link](https://apnews.com/article/supreme-court-immigration-trump-d36d0092617c7115780c06de38e2000f)

**Zelenskyy says Russia pulling air defenses to Moscow and Putin's residence after Ukrainian drone strikes.** Ukraine struck two oil refineries in Ufa, 1,500 kilometers from the front, and an oil depot in Krasnodar overnight. Zelenskyy said Russia has massed hundreds of launchers in the Moscow region and redeployed roughly 90 launchers to Valdai, site of a Putin residence, from other Russian regions. He added that Belarus has switched off drone relay stations following his ultimatum, though Belarus continues building military storage infrastructure on the Ukrainian border. [link](https://apnews.com/article/russia-ukraine-war-drones-crimea-trump-25dc47db4d1a3b2908b8f060434aa765)

**Slate Auto prices its no-frills electric pickup at $24,950, opens preorders with 180,000 reservations.** The Bezos-backed startup set a $300 non-refundable deposit requirement and scheduled production to begin in Warsaw, Indiana, in autumn 2026. The truck omits a touchscreen, stereo, and power windows. Specs improved from initial targets: range reached 205 miles, tow rating doubled to 2,000 pounds, and payload rose to 1,550 pounds. The average new vehicle sold for $49,220 in May; the average new EV averaged $54,532. [link](https://arstechnica.com/features/2026/06/we-take-a-ride-in-slates-24950-electric-pickup)

**Venezuela doublet explained: a 7.2 and 7.5 struck 39 seconds apart on a fault comparable to San Andreas.** USGS says the quakes ruptured where the Caribbean plate moves eastward past the South American plate at about 0.79 inches per year. The Bocono fault last produced a doublet in September 2025. Venezuela has no earthquake early-warning system. USGS puts the probability of at least one magnitude-4 aftershock within a week at 99 percent, and a magnitude-6 aftershock at 24 percent. [link](https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-earthquakes-doublet-f61cc9b92ba4e0735cfed6391c21e4fd)

**Anthropic reports AI now writes 80% of its code; researcher Mollick announces new book on coexistence with superior AI.** Anthropic disclosed that each developer now ships eight times more code than before, with AI authoring 80 percent of it. Wharton professor Ethan Mollick, whose AI book sold in 25-plus languages, announced a follow-up titled Co-Existence, due October 20, arguing that AI is 'sometimes, but not always, better than you.' He used an AI-run A/B testing process to optimize the book's website for both human and AI readers. [link](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/co-existence-and-the-end-of-co-intelligence)

**Samsung narrowly averts strike of 48,000 workers demanding share of AI semiconductor profits.** Samsung agreed to abolish its bonus cap and link bonuses to operating profit, setting aside about 10.5 percent of chip-division operating profit for special bonuses. The union had demanded 15 percent across all divisions. The dispute followed record profits driven by memory chip demand from Nvidia and Tesla. A South Korean presidential policy chief proposed a broader 'citizen's dividend' from AI-era excess profits before the deal was reached. [link](https://restofworld.org/2026/samsung-south-korea-union-ai-profits)
