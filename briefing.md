**Israel and Lebanon sign U.S.-brokered framework agreement; Hezbollah excluded, warns of civil war.** Israeli Ambassador Yechiel Leiter and Lebanese Ambassador Nada Hamadeh Moawad signed a trilateral framework agreement at the State Department on Friday, with Secretary of State Rubio presiding. The U.S. committed $100 million in humanitarian assistance and will facilitate a new Military Coordination Group for Lebanon. Hezbollah was excluded from the talks. A Hezbollah parliamentary official warned Lebanese authorities cannot enforce the agreement "unless they go, with American support, to civil war." **Trump administration asks Supreme Court to approve mandatory ICE detention for long-term U.S. residents.** Solicitor General John Sauer filed Friday urging the Supreme Court to resolve a circuit split over whether people living in the U.S. for years or decades must be detained without bond during deportation proceedings. Federal district courts have issued more than 9,300 rulings against the policy. Five appeals circuits have ruled 3-2 against the administration. The policy, adopted July 8, 2025, treats interior-apprehended individuals as equivalent to border crossers. [link](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/06/26/trump-ice-detention-supreme-court-00978482)

The partial reinstatement comes two weeks after an export control directive required Anthropic to disable both Mythos and Fable 5. Fable 5 remains unavailable; Anthropic says talks with the White House over Fable 5 are continuing through the weekend. [link](https://www.wired.com/story/anthropic-restores-access-to-mythos)

**OpenAI delays GPT-5.6 public release at White House request.** OpenAI confirmed Friday it is delaying the public release of GPT-5.6, which comes in three variants named Sol, Terra, and Luna, at the Trump administration's request. The company will first share the models with a government-preapproved set of customers, then work with the administration to expand access. OpenAI wrote it does not believe "this kind of government access process should become the long-term default." [link](https://www.wired.com/story/openai-gpt-56-model-release-trump-admin-approval)

**Venezuela doublet earthquakes kill more than 180; 1,500 injured, thousands missing.** A magnitude 7.2 quake struck Venezuela's northern coast Wednesday evening, followed 39 seconds later by a magnitude 7.5. The coastal region of La Guaira, north of Caracas, sustained the heaviest casualties. Some 1,500 people were injured and thousands reported missing. The USGS said there is a 99% probability of at least one magnitude 4 aftershock within the next week and a 24% chance of a magnitude 6.
